Scenario: Slips, Trips & Falls An elderly gentleman is coming into the treatment room. He goes to put his hand on the bench to steady himself but misses and falls, hitting his head on the bench.

  • Complete a formal Incident Report Form.
  • Identify the hazard (slippery floor? clutter?) to prevent recurrence.
Scenario: Sharps Injury (Instrument) While reaching across the instrument tray during a procedure, your hand brushes against a contaminated scaler. The sharp tip penetrates your glove and breaks the skin.

What do you do?
  • Stop immediately and verify the injury.
  • Encourage the wound to bleed gently; wash with soap and warm running water. Do not scrub.
  • Cover with a waterproof dressing.
  • Notify your supervisor/dentist immediately.
  • Follow the practice's Sharps Protocol (requires medical assessment and blood tests for you and potentially the patient).
  • Complete an Incident Report detailing the specific instrument involved.
Scenario: Swallowed Object During the removal of a temporary crown, the crown dislodges unexpectedly and drops into the patient's throat. The patient coughs and appears to swallow it.

What do you do?
  • Sit the patient up and encourage coughing.
  • Notify the Dentist immediately.
  • If the object is lost/swallowed, the patient must be referred for an immediate X-ray to rule out aspiration into the lungs.
  • Inform the patient of the incident (Duty of Candour/Open Disclosure).
  • Document the event thoroughly in patient notes.
  • Complete an Incident Report.
